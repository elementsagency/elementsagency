Websites & design

Word of mouth is great — but it has a ceiling. The moment someone hears about your business, the first thing they do is Google you. If they find nothing, or find something that looks outdated, you lose them before they ever pick up the phone.

A website works for you 24/7 — answering questions, building trust and generating enquiries even while you're busy doing the actual work. It also opens up SEO and paid ads, which can bring in customers who've never heard of you at all.

Most business websites are ready within 4–6 weeks from the day we start. E-commerce stores or larger projects typically take 8–12 weeks.

SEO & Google

SEO (Search Engine Optimisation) is what helps your business show up on Google when people search for what you offer. Think "plumber in London" or "best restaurant Canterbury" — SEO is how you get into those results.

If you want customers to find you without paying for ads every month, yes — you need it. It takes time to build but the results last. We set up solid SEO foundations on every website we build, and offer ongoing SEO as a separate monthly service if you want to rank higher and faster.

Honestly — it takes time. Most businesses start seeing meaningful results in 3–6 months. Local SEO (getting found in your area) tends to be faster than national SEO.

Anyone who promises you page 1 of Google in a week is not being honest with you. We'll tell you exactly what to expect based on your industry and location.

Local SEO focuses on getting your business found by people searching in your area. It includes optimising your Google Business profile, building local citations and targeting location-based keywords like "electrician in Canterbury".

For most small businesses — restaurants, trades, garages, local services — local SEO is more valuable than trying to rank nationally. It gets you in front of people who are ready to buy, nearby.

Yes — every website we build includes on-page SEO foundations as standard. This means proper page structure, meta tags, fast loading speed and mobile optimisation — all the technical basics Google needs to index your site correctly.

Ongoing SEO (monthly content, link building, ranking improvements) is a separate service you can add on top.

Paid ads & marketing

Google Ads puts you at the top of search results immediately — but you pay each time someone clicks, and the moment you stop paying, you disappear. SEO builds your ranking organically over time — it's slower to start but free traffic once it's working.

For most businesses, the best approach is both — ads to get results quickly while SEO builds in the background for long-term growth.

It depends on your industry, location and goals. For most local businesses, a starting budget of £300–£500/month for ads is enough to test and see results. We manage the ad budget separately from our management fee.

We'll always tell you our honest recommendation for your situation — we're not going to push you to spend more than makes sense.

Yes, we can manage ads for an existing website. However, if your website isn't in good shape — slow, confusing or not built to convert visitors — ads will underperform. We'll always be honest with you about this before spending your budget.
